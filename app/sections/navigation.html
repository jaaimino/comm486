Navigation

Usage

Navigation usually takes the role of how users get between different "pages" or sections of your web application. In this section, I am mostly referencing the main site navigation, and not any sub navigation menus for changing sections within a page.

Examples

Here are some example navbar styles that are often seen on the modern web.

Top Navigation

In this style, links are aligned horizontally in a list, usually in order or importance. Here, the most important links go to the left.

Side Navigation

Here, we have links stacked vertically, in order of importance. Most often, we have the most important links at the top, and least important at the bottom.
